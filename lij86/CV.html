    AMO/Quantum Information

    Advisor: Dr. Samir Bali, Professor of Physics

    • Non-volatile Atomic Memory in Raman Scattering of Photons from Warm Alkali Atoms (10/2020-present)
      -Investigated a novel nonvolatile warm-alkali atomic memory entangled with Raman scattered single photons with home-built photon counting machine with FPGA.

    • Extra-dimensional Fidelity Enhancement of Stored Qubits in Photons with OAM (07/2020-present)
      -Discovered and investigated a novel approach to store photons used as qubits with immunity to atomic diffusions in near room temperature

    • Storage of Twisted Light and Arbitrary Images in Atomic Coherences (09/2019-08/2020)
      -Verified experimentally the topological stability of twisted light stored in atomic coherences and the time evolution of images in this storage.
      -Derived the theory for the evolution of the profile of the stored electric field in atomic coherences. Link

    • Dicke Narrowing of Varied Atomic Localizations in Electromagnetically induced Transparency (03/2019-09/2019)
      -Designed the experiment and verified the theory of Dicke Narrowing in EIT and studied its significance in the context of quantum information.
      -Derived the theory that the original author of this topic used. Link
      -Work will be submitted to Optics Letters as the second author (equally contributed with the first author).

    • Producing Slow Light in Warm Alkali Vapor using Electromagnetically induced Transparency (09/2018-03/2019)
      -Created the slowest light pulse in the lab (59 m/s, 03/2020) with pulse compression mechanism to be addressed.
      -Work submitted to American Journal of Physics as a co-author.
